Why UI/UX Matters

💡 Real-World Good UI/UX

  • Apple: Consistent design. Feels polished, works intuitively.
  • Duolingo: Gamified language learning. Addictive and simple.
  • Google Search: One bar, no distractions. Clear goal.

😖 Bad UI/UX in the Wild

  • 🛑 Government forms with 25+ fields on one screen.
  • 🌀 Websites that attack you with 8 pop-ups before you can scroll.
  • 🎯 Captchas that ask you to click every crosswalk — again.
🔵 Google’s 41 Shades of Blue

Google tested 41 different link colors. One shade led to more clicks and earned them $200 million in extra ad revenue annually.

Source: Business Insider
🏠 Airbnb's Photos Doubled Bookings

Airbnb swapped bad photos for high-res ones. Bookings doubled — because great UX builds trust.

Source: Airbnb Design
👻 Snapchat’s Redesign Fail

Snapchat changed its UI in 2018. Over a million users signed a petition against it. The backlash wiped $1.3 billion off their stock.

Source: CNN, Change.org
🍔 McDonald's Kiosk Accessibility

Touchscreen kiosks looked sleek — but lacked accessibility features. Good UI, but a bad UX for many users.

Source: The Guardian

📊 Quick Stats That Prove the Point

  • 88% of users won’t return after a bad experience.
  • 70% of shopping carts are abandoned due to poor UX.
  • Better button design helped Amazon make $300M more.

🧭 Good Design Depends on Context

  • Instagram should feel fun and visual.
  • A hospital app should feel calm and secure.
  • A bank site? Maybe even a little boring — but reliable.

🎯 3 Tips for Better UI/UX

  1. Keep it simple — remove clutter and confusion.
  2. Make it obvious — buttons and instructions should be clear.
  3. Test with people — watch where they struggle, then fix it.
